The SERVIR App Center is a dynamic inventory of applications developed under the SERVIR Program, either by regional hubs and their partners, Applied Science Team (AST) projects or the Science Coordination Office (SCO). The applications on the App Center can be filtered by regions or by service areas to facilitate their discovery. The detail page for each application presents technical information and credits to all parties involved in their design, development and implementation. This inventory continues to grow as new tools are developed.

In the spirit of supporting Open Science, SERVIR partners make the underlying code for these apps available through public repositories. The information in this inventory is maintained by the SERVIR SCO. The individual applications and their associated datasets are maintained by their respective implementation teams.

SERVIR is a joint initiative of NASA, USAID, and leading geospatial organizations in Asia, Africa, and Latin America. The SERVIR program partners with countries and organizations to address critical challenges in Agriculture and Food Security, Ecosystem and Carbon Management, Water Security, Weather & Climate Resilience and Air Quality and Health.

For more information on the SERVIR program, visit https://servirglobal.net
